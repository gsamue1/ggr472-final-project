Food Support Information

Here is your place for all things food support!
Any frequently asking questions (FAQs) about accessing services and descriptions of all the service types can be found below!

What are the different types of food support services?

Food Banks

Nonprofit organizations that collect safe, nutritious and, often unprepared, food, and redistributes it to individuals and families experiencing food insecurity.

Takeout Meals Programs

Services that allow for drop-in ready-to-eat takeaway meals. Examples: sandwich and snack distribution.

Sit Down Meal Programs

These are services that offer individuals the space to ‘dine-in’ and eat with others. Meals may vary from site to site, but one example of this service would be a soup kitchen.

Community Programming

These programs are focused on providing indviduals with skills and spaces to cook nutritious meals. Programming includes community kitchens where you can cook with others, nutrition programs and support groups for individuals suffering from food insecurity. To find out what programs a location is offering, check out their website linked in the Pop-Up window on the map!

Community Fridges and Gardens

These are free and accessible fridges and gardens in public spaces that are operated by and for the community. Necessities such as fresh produce, whole meals, pantry goods, and personal care items can be accessed by anyone, at any time of day, no questions asked, without enrolling in a program, and free from the stigma associated with food insecurity. Runs by a “take what you need, leave what you can” motto.

What should I bring with me when accessing these services?

Each food service is different! Notably, community fridges will often have no requirements while community programs or food banks will often have the most. You should check the specific requirements on each website.

Typically you can expect to bring:

1. Grocery Bags, Boxes or a Cart
2. Groverment Issued Photo ID
3. Proof of address
4. Documentation of your most recent monthly income
5. If you have an appointment booked and were provided a slip, bring your appointment slip

What are residency requirements?

Residency requirements means that a food support service only provides their services to people living within a specific area. If you do not have a current address, always reach out to the organization as they do make exceptions in some circumstances. Residency requirements will either be explained as a postal code or as an area contained by roads.
Example 1: Catchment Area Depicted by Roads -- Eglinton Ave E (north) to O’Connor Dr (south), Bayview Ave (west) to Victoria Park Ave (east)
Example 2: Catachment Area by Postal Code -- Must be living in postal code starting with "M4Y" (Check the mail of wherever you are staying)

Who should I contact if I am having trouble finding the right service for me?

If you have having trouble finding a service that works from you try reaching out to Help Seeker. They are an organization aimed at connecting people around Toronto with the right food support services for them! You can also try pluging in your needs into ChatGPT for tailored results! For example, "Can you give me a list of food banks that serve the postal code M3N in Toronto that are open on Thursdays?"
