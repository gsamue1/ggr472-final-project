Food Support Information

Here is your place for all things food support!
Any frequently asking questions (FAQs) about accessing services and descriptions of all the service types can be found below!

Food Banks

Nonprofit organizations that collect safe, nutritious and culturally appropriate food, and redistributes it to individuals and families experiencing food insecurity

Community Fridges and Gardens

These are free and accessible fridges and gardens in public spaces that are operated by and for the community. Necessities such as fresh produce, whole meals, pantry goods, and personal care items can be accessed by anyone, at any time of day, no questions asked, without enrolling in a program, and free from the stigma associated with food insecurity. Runs by a “take what you need, leave what you can” motto.

Community Programming

These are programs that work to ensure food banks have the resources, capacity, training, and support to best serve their community’s unique needs. Many community programs are specific to each neighborhood.

Takeout Programs

Services that allow for drop-in ready-to-eat takeaway meals.

Sit Down (Soup Kitchen)

These are services that offer individuals the space to ‘dine-in’ and eat with others.

Residency Requirements

This is the eligibility criteria of residents who wish to access a certain food service location. They need to ensure that they live within those residency boundaries/catchment areas. E.g Eglinton Ave E (north) to O’Connor Dr (south), Bayview Ave (west) to Victoria Park Ave (east)

What should I bring with me when accessing these services?

You should check the specific requirements on each website as each food service is different in a neighborhood but typically you can expect to bring: Photo ID, Proof of address, Documentation of your most recent monthly income, Grocery bags and/or carts, Appointment slip
